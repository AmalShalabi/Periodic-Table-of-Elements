Alkali Metals

Description: Alkali metals are highly reactive, soft metals with low melting points. They readily form ions with a +1 charge.

Atomic Radius: Increases down the group.

Reactivity: Most reactive metals; react vigorously with water to form hydroxides and hydrogen gas.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Lithium (Li) Reacts slowly with water; forms lithium hydroxide 152 Solid
Sodium (Na) Reacts vigorously with water; forms sodium hydroxide 186 Solid
Potassium (K) Reacts explosively with water; forms potassium hydroxide 231 Solid

Alkaline Earth Metals

Description: Alkaline earth metals are reactive but less so than alkali metals. They are harder and have higher melting points.

Atomic Radius: Increases down the group.

Reactivity: Less reactive than alkali metals; react with water to form hydroxides and hydrogen gas.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Magnesium (Mg) Reacts slowly with water; forms magnesium hydroxide 160 Solid
Calcium (Ca) Reacts moderately with water; forms calcium hydroxide 197 Solid
Barium (Ba) Reacts slowly with water; forms barium hydroxide 217 Solid

Transition Metals

Description: Transition metals are characterized by their variable oxidation states and the formation of colored compounds. They often exhibit catalytic properties and are less reactive than alkali and alkaline earth metals.

Atomic Radius: Variable, but generally decreases across a period.

Reactivity: Less reactive compared to alkali and alkaline earth metals; form stable oxides.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Iron (Fe) Reacts slowly with oxygen; forms iron oxides 156 Solid
Copper (Cu) Reacts slowly with oxygen and moisture; forms copper oxides 145 Solid
Silver (Ag) Reacts slowly with sulfur compounds; tarnishes in air 145 Solid

Lanthanides

Description: Lanthanides are a series of 15 metallic elements with atomic numbers 57 to 71. They are similar in properties to lanthanum and exhibit variable oxidation states.

Atomic Radius: Relatively uniform throughout the series.

Reactivity: Lanthanides are reactive but less so than alkali and alkaline earth metals.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Cerium (Ce) Reacts slowly with oxygen; forms cerium oxides 182 Solid
Neodymium (Nd) Reacts slowly with water; forms neodymium hydroxide 206 Solid
Lutetium (Lu) Reacts slowly with acids; forms lutetium oxide 175 Solid

Actinides

Description: Actinides are a series of 15 metallic elements with atomic numbers 89 to 103. They are radioactive and exhibit various oxidation states.

Atomic Radius: Varied due to the presence of the actinide series.

Reactivity: Actinides are reactive metals with diverse chemical properties.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Uranium (U) Reacts slowly with oxygen; forms uranium oxides 186 Solid
Plutonium (Pu) Reacts slowly with water and oxygen; forms plutonium oxides 175 Solid
Thorium (Th) Reacts slowly with acids; forms thorium oxide 179 Solid

Metalloids

Description: Metalloids have properties intermediate between metals and nonmetals. They are semiconductors and exhibit characteristics of both metals and nonmetals.

Atomic Radius: Variable, depending on the specific metalloid.

Reactivity: Generally less reactive compared to metals; behavior varies.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Silicon (Si) Reacts with oxygen; forms silicon dioxide 117 Solid
Arsenic (As) Reacts with oxygen and halogens; forms arsenic oxides and halides 114 Solid
Germanium (Ge) Reacts with oxygen; forms germanium dioxide 125 Solid

Post-Transition Metals

Description: Post-transition metals are softer and have lower melting points compared to transition metals. They exhibit lower electronegativity and are less reactive.

Atomic Radius: Variable, but generally larger than transition metals.

Reactivity: Generally less reactive than transition metals; form stable compounds.

Element Reactivity Atomic Radius (pm) Room Temperature State
Aluminum (Al) Reacts slowly with oxygen to form aluminum oxide 143 Solid
Lead (Pb) Reacts slowly with oxygen; forms lead oxide 154 Solid
Tin (Sn) Reacts slowly with oxygen and acids; forms tin oxide 140 Solid

Atomic Radius: Decreases down the group.

Noble Gases

Description: Noble gases are inert, colorless, and odorless gases under standard conditions. They have very low chemical reactivity due to their complete valence electron shell.

Atomic Radius: Increases down the group.

Reactivity: Extremely low reactivity; under certain conditions, some noble gases can form compounds.
