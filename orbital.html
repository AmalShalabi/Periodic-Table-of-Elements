Understanding Electron Orbitals

What is an Orbital?

An atomic orbital is a mathematical function that describes the wave-like behavior of an electron in an atom. This function can be used to calculate the probability of finding an electron in a specific region around the nucleus.

Orbitals are categorized by their shapes and are named as s, p, d, and f orbitals. Each type of orbital has a unique shape:

  • s-orbital: Spherical shape.
  • p-orbital: Dumbbell shape.
  • d-orbital: Clover shape.
  • f-orbital: Complex shapes.
